# Data Breach Notification Template

**Effective Date:** December 27, 2025  
**Last Updated:** December 27, 2025  
**Entity:** ERMITS LLC (VendorSoluce)

## Purpose

This template provides a standardized format for notifying users of data breaches, ensuring compliance with:
- State breach notification laws (all 50 states)
- FTC Section 5 (unfair or deceptive practices)
- GDPR (72-hour notification requirement)
- Other applicable privacy laws

---

## When to Use This Template

**Notification Required When:**
- Unauthorized access to personal information has occurred
- Personal information has been acquired by unauthorized person
- Personal information has been accessed in an unauthorized manner
- There is a reasonable likelihood of harm to affected individuals

**Notification Timeline:**
- **GDPR:** Within 72 hours of discovery
- **State Laws:** Without unreasonable delay (typically 30-60 days)
- **FTC:** As soon as reasonably possible

**Note:** Consult legal counsel before sending breach notifications to ensure compliance with all applicable laws.

## Breach Notification Template

### Email Subject Line

```
[URGENT] Important Security Notice Regarding Your VendorSoluce Account
```

### Email Body Template

```
Subject: Important Security Notice Regarding Your VendorSoluce Account

Dear [Customer Name],

We are writing to inform you of a security incident that may have affected your VendorSoluce account.

WHAT HAPPENED

On [DATE], we discovered [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF INCIDENT]. We immediately began an investigation and took steps to secure our systems.

WHAT INFORMATION WAS INVOLVED

The following types of information may have been affected:
- [LIST SPECIFIC TYPES OF INFORMATION, e.g., "Email addresses, names, account preferences"]
- [DO NOT INCLUDE: Information that was NOT affected]

WHAT WE ARE DOING

We have taken the following steps in response to this incident:
1. [ACTION TAKEN, e.g., "Secured affected systems and closed the security vulnerability"]
2. [ACTION TAKEN, e.g., "Engaged a third-party security firm to investigate"]
3. [ACTION TAKEN, e.g., "Notified law enforcement"]
4. [ACTION TAKEN, e.g., "Enhanced security measures to prevent future incidents"]

WHAT YOU CAN DO

To protect your account, we recommend that you:
1. [RECOMMENDATION, e.g., "Change your password immediately"]
2. [RECOMMENDATION, e.g., "Enable multi-factor authentication if not already enabled"]
3. [RECOMMENDATION, e.g., "Monitor your account for any suspicious activity"]
4. [RECOMMENDATION, e.g., "Review your account settings and recent activity"]

[IF IDENTITY THEFT PROTECTION OFFERED:]
We are offering [IDENTITY THEFT PROTECTION SERVICE] at no cost to you for [DURATION]. To enroll, please [INSTRUCTIONS].

FOR MORE INFORMATION

If you have questions about this incident, please contact us:
- Email: security@ermits.com
- Phone: [PHONE NUMBER]
- Subject: "Security Incident Inquiry - [INCIDENT ID]"

We take the security of your information seriously and sincerely apologize for any inconvenience this may cause.

Sincerely,
ERMITS LLC Security Team
```

## Breach Notification Checklist

Before sending breach notifications:

- [ ] Incident has been contained and systems secured
- [ ] Scope of breach has been determined
- [ ] Affected individuals have been identified
- [ ] Types of information involved have been documented
- [ ] Legal counsel has been consulted
- [ ] Relevant state authorities have been notified (if required)
- [ ] Credit reporting agencies have been notified (if required)
- [ ] Identity theft protection has been arranged (if applicable)
- [ ] Notification template has been customized with specific details
- [ ] Contact information for questions has been provided
- [ ] Timeline for notification has been met (72 hours for GDPR, state law deadlines)

---

## Required Information Elements

Every breach notification must include:

1. **What Happened:**
   - Clear description of the incident
   - Date/time of discovery
   - Date/time of incident (if known)

2. **What Information Was Involved:**
   - Specific types of information affected
   - What information was NOT affected

3. **What We Are Doing:**
   - Steps taken to secure systems
   - Remediation measures
   - Prevention measures

4. **What You Can Do:**
   - Specific steps users should take
   - How to protect themselves
   - Where to get help

5. **Contact Information:**
   - How to ask questions
   - How to report suspicious activity
   - How to get additional help

6. **Timeline:**
   - When the incident occurred
   - When it was discovered
   - When users are being notified

---

## Prohibited Information

**DO NOT Include:**
- Specific technical details that could help attackers
- Information about ongoing investigation (if it could compromise security)
- Speculation about cause or perpetrators
- Information that could cause panic
- Unverified information

## Notification Methods

**Primary Method:**
- Email to registered email address
- In-app notification (if applicable)

**Secondary Methods (if email unavailable):**
- Postal mail to registered address
- Phone call (for high-risk breaches)
- Public notice (if contact information unavailable)

---

## Incident Response Process

1. **Discovery:** Incident is discovered
2. **Containment:** Immediate steps to secure systems
3. **Investigation:** Determine scope and impact
4. **Legal Review:** Consult legal counsel
5. **Notification Preparation:** Customize template
6. **Regulatory Notification:** Notify authorities (if required)
7. **User Notification:** Send notifications to affected users
8. **Remediation:** Implement fixes and improvements
9. **Documentation:** Document incident and response
10. **Follow-up:** Monitor for additional issues
